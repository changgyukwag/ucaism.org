Purpose

A serious space for ideas before final publication

Working Papers allow UCAISM.org to function like a research institution rather than a static author page. They create a visible place for arguments that are mature enough to share, but still open to refinement, evidence, and scholarly dialogue.

This layer sits between Foundation Papers and Policy Briefs: deeper than a brief, more provisional than a final paper, and useful for testing institutional design concepts before formal publication.

Research method

How Working Papers should function

Working Papers should be serious, transparent, and provisional. Their role is to invite refinement without weakening the authority of the published platform.

Versioned

Each paper can carry a version date so updates are clear and citation-friendly.

Structured

Every draft should include abstract, research question, argument, implications, and status.

Connected

Working Papers should link upward to Foundation Papers and outward to Policy Briefs.

Careful

Claims should remain legally and factually safe, especially when discussing companies, cases, or policy disputes.

Publication pipeline

Recommended progression

This page creates a visible research pipeline that can grow into a full citation and publication ecosystem.

1. Working Paper

Draft argument, test structure, gather evidence, and clarify definitions.

2. Foundation Paper

Convert mature arguments into durable long-form research documents.

3. Policy Brief

Translate mature ideas into action-oriented guidance for wider audiences.
