Purpose

A research spine for the UCAISM platform

Foundation Papers are designed to give UCAISM.org a scholarly backbone. They sit between the public-facing book project and future policy briefs, working papers, citations, events, and media resources.

Each paper should define one major concept clearly enough for academic readers, policy audiences, journalists, and institutional partners to understand the architecture without depending on the full book manuscript.

Editorial structure

How the series should work

The Foundation Papers page is built as a durable institutional hub. It can begin with planned papers and mature into a formal publication archive as PDFs, abstracts, citations, and downloadable assets become available.

Abstract

Each paper should have a 120–180 word abstract that explains the research question, core claim, and relevance.

Working draft

Draft papers can be marked as working versions before final PDFs are added to the Downloads Center.

Citation

Each released paper can later connect to Phase D-6 Citation Center with approved citation formats.

Policy bridge

Major foundation papers can generate shorter Phase D-3 policy briefs for public and institutional audiences.

Next platform layer

From foundation research to policy translation

Phase D-2 establishes the long-form intellectual architecture. Phase D-3 should translate selected ideas into concise, action-oriented policy briefs.

  • Keep Foundation Papers scholarly, conceptual, and durable.
  • Keep Policy Briefs shorter, more actionable, and easier for policymakers and media readers to scan.
  • Use the Research Library as the central discovery layer connecting both formats.
