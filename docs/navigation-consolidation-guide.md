# Navigation Consolidation Guide

The site now has enough content to look like a research platform. The risk is not lack of content; the risk is navigation overload.

## Recommended top-level navigation

Use a compact top-level menu:

1. Home
2. Research
3. Books
4. Insights
5. Media
6. Community
7. Contact

## Recommended grouping

### Research
- Research Library
- Foundation Papers
- Policy Briefs
- Working Papers
- Citation Center

### Books
- Open Wins
- Intelligent Capitalism
- Publications Center
- Downloads Center

### Insights
- News & Insights
- Newsletter
- Events

### Media
- Media Kit
- Author Bio
- Press Resources

### Community
- Global Community
- Newsletter
- Events

## Reader-first rule

The top menu should not expose every page at once. A global reader should understand where to begin within five seconds.

## Suggested homepage pathway block

Add a lightweight “Explore UCAISM” section only if the homepage feels unclear:

- Read the book
- Explore the research
- Cite the work
- Follow updates
- Join the community

## Avoid

- More than seven top-level navigation items
- Repeating every Phase D page in the header
- Long dropdowns on mobile
- Adding external services before the content architecture is stable
