# Phase E-4 Translation Style Notes

UCAISM.org should not treat multilingual expansion as mechanical translation. The goal is to preserve institutional seriousness, authorial rhythm, and conceptual precision across languages.

## Core rules

1. Keep UCAISM as the global brand term.
2. Translate explanatory subtitles, not the brand identity itself.
3. Preserve key terms such as individual sovereignty, fiduciary AI, open rails, digital public infrastructure, and Intelligent Capitalism with stable terminology in each language.
4. Avoid overly promotional language. The tone should remain research-oriented, public-facing, and globally readable.
5. Mark translated pages as foundation, draft, reviewed, or final.
6. English remains the source of truth unless a translated page is explicitly marked final.

## Initial language order

1. Korean — author-origin and domestic public-intellectual audience.
2. Japanese — regional public-policy and digital-governance audience.
3. Spanish — broad global audience across Europe and Latin America.
4. French, German, Italian, and Portuguese can follow after core pages stabilize.
